What Is Reiki Energy Healing?

Exploring the roots and benefits of this gentle energy practice.

Origins of Reiki

Reiki is an energy healing practice that traces its roots to early 20th‑century Japan. The term comes from the Japanese words rei, meaning universal, and ki, meaning vital life force energy【219368461226737†L75-L83】. Practitioners believe they can channel this universal energy by lightly placing their hands on or just above a person’s body in a series of hand positions【219368461226737†L75-L83】.

Techniques taught in Reiki training include centering, clearing, beaming and smoothing the aura【219368461226737†L75-L90】. Rather than “giving” you their own energy, Reiki practitioners see themselves as conduits for the life force energy that exists all around us【219368461226737†L91-L93】.

How does Reiki work?

The practice of Reiki is based on the idea that encouraging the flow of universal life force energy through your body can help create a healthier internal environment【219368461226737†L140-L148】. According to this theory, stagnant or blocked energy may contribute to pain and illness, while free‑flowing energy supports balance and well‑being【219368461226737†L140-L149】.

While scientific evidence is limited, many people report feeling deeply relaxed after a session. Some use Reiki alongside conventional treatments to support healing and ease stress. It’s important to remember that Reiki is complementary and not a replacement for medical care【219368461226737†L140-L168】.

Potential benefits

People seek Reiki for a variety of reasons. The Cleveland Clinic notes that practitioners and clients claim Reiki can help with:

  • Better sleep【219368461226737†L140-L158】
  • Reduced anxiety and boosted mood【219368461226737†L140-L158】
  • Decreased pain【219368461226737†L140-L158】
  • Increased energy and improved circulation【219368461226737†L140-L158】

It’s worth noting that research on Reiki is still emerging and some studies have small sample sizes【219368461226737†L160-L186】. Nevertheless, many people find value in the sense of calm and connection they experience during a session. Trust your own experience and work with practitioners who make you feel safe and respected.
